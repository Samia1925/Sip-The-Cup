world war to Americano by admin / 21st may, 2059

The invention of the Americano coffee is often traced back to World War II. American soldiers stationed in Italy found the local espresso too strong for their taste. To make it more palatable, they started adding hot water to dilute the espresso, creating a milder coffee more akin to the drip coffee they were used to back home.

expression of expresso by admin / 21st may, 2059

The word "espresso" means "pressed-out" in Italian, referring to the method of forcing hot water through finely-ground coffee under pressure. The desire to brew coffee more quickly was key to its invention. Before espresso machines, coffee was typically made using slower methods, such as boiling or percolating.

Donut Diversity by admin / 21st may, 2059

Fried dough has been a part of human cuisine for centuries, with variations found in many cultures. Ancient Greeks and Romans made fried dough pastries sweetened with honey, while the Dutch brought their own version of fried dough, called olykoeks ("oil cakes"), to the American colonies in the 1600s. These early doughnuts were simple, sometimes filled with fruit or nuts, and fried in oil.
